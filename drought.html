Drought & Climate

Droughts occur when rainfall is consistently below average over long periods. This lack of water stresses ecosystems, reduces crop yields, dries up rivers and lakes, and creates extremely dry conditions that fuel wildfires. With climate change, droughts are becoming longer and more severe, increasing the risk of destructive fire seasons that harm biodiversity, air quality, and human health.
