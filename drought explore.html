1. How low rainfall causes drought

When rainfall is much lower than normal for weeks to years, it creates a water deficit. This leads to:

  • Reduced rainfall lowers soil moisture and groundwater.
  • Rivers, lakes, and reservoirs dry up.
  • Plants and crops cannot grow properly.

Together, this prolonged shortage of water creates a drought.

2. How droughts trigger wildfires

  • Dry vegetation becomes like fuel for fire.
  • Lack of water makes controlling fires harder.
  • Hot winds spread flames rapidly.

Drought-stricken areas are highly prone to wildfires.
